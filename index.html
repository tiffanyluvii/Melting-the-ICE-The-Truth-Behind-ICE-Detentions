In 2024, following Donald Trump’s election to the presidency, concerns were raised by critics about a rise in xenophobic sentiment and increased targeting of individuals perceived to be immigrants, particularly people of color who were sometimes assumed to be undocumented based on appearance alone

One policy frequently referenced in this context is Executive Order 14159 titled "Protecting the American People Against Invasion". Supporters describe it as strengthening immigration enforcement, while critics argue that it contributed to broader and more aggressive enforcement practices, including expanded authority for agencies such as ICE.





This raises the question: what is ICE’s role?





U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) is responsible for enforcing immigration laws, investigating transnational crime, and preventing the illegal movement of people and goods across borders.

Supporters of Trump claim that ICE have done a wonderful job in enforcing immigration laws and protecting the country from illegal immigrants. They are also supportive of the use of force and the treatment of immigrants.

It's interesting to note that ICE has been praised for its work in enforcing immigration laws and protecting the country from illegal immigrants. This is despite the fact that there is publicly available footage showing the extent of these operations against suspected immigrants.

Well, why is it that immigrants have been treated as below human beings?

Critics argue that part of the answer lies in fear-based political messaging. Throughout the Trump administration, there have been many instances of fear-based political messaging. The president has referred to immigrants as "animals", "criminals", "security threats", "bad genes", "garbage", "stone cold killers", and other derogatory terms. He has also used the media to spread fear and misinformation about immigrants to convince the public that they are a threat to the country. This has led to a climate of fear and suspicion towards immigrants, which has been used to justify the use of force and the treatment of immigrants.
